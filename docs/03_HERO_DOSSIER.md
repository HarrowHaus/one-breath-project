## The hero: Allen Benedict Court

**What happened (publicly reported).** In January 2019, at Allen Benedict Court, a public housing complex in Columbia, South Carolina, two residents — **Calvin Witherspoon Jr.** and **Derrick Caldwell Roper** — died from carbon monoxide poisoning. Local authorities found that **none of the apartments had carbon monoxide detectors.** About 400 residents were evacuated. A survivor, Marcus Ballard, called 911 for himself after his cousin stumbled into his room; no one suspected CO until the two neighbors were found dead the next day. Residents and the victims' families sued the Columbia Housing Authority, alleging failure to maintain the property.

**Why it changed things.** Public housing was not required to have CO detectors under federal law — HUD required smoke detectors in subsidized housing, but not CO alarms. After an NBC News investigation exposed the gap, **HUD announced it was drafting the first federal rule requiring CO detectors in public housing**, and later **set a deadline to install carbon monoxide alarms in about 3 million units nationwide.**

**Human anchor (single face for the hero).** Weeks after his death, **Witherspoon's daughter, Dani Washington, still reflexively dialed her father's number in the mornings, only to reach his voicemail.** A resident, Sharon Johnson, said she'd been warned about lead, mold, and mildew, but never carbon monoxide: "I knew it was a gas, but I didn't know it was a gas that could kill us."

**Why this hero:** unambiguously preventable (a missing alarm); adult victims (the anchor isn't built on a child); on-thesis (rental/institutional negligence — hits renters and landlords at once); and it carries the efficacy payoff — two men died and advocacy forced federal change.

**Sources:** NBC News, "Carbon monoxide is killing public housing residents, but HUD doesn't require detectors" and "'How many more people have to die?'" (Suzy Khimm, 2019); CBS News, "Carbon monoxide detectors save lives. Why aren't they required everywhere?" (2022). Verify all figures/quotes against the originals before publishing.
