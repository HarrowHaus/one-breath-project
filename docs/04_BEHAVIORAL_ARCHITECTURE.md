# 04 — Behavioral Architecture

The site is engineered on the science of why people ignore invisible risk and what moves them to act. These are design mandates, not vibes.

## Why CO defeats normal awareness (the apathy diagnosis)
1. **No affect signal.** Humans judge risk emotionally first; CO is odorless/colorless/tasteless, so it generates no instinctive fear. (The biggest driver.)
2. **Optimism bias / low base rate.** Rare-but-catastrophic risk gets under-weighted: "not me."
3. **Symptom misattribution.** Early poisoning mimics flu; people rationalize it away.
4. **False-security belief (seniors).** A UL survey found ~77% of older Americans believe they could sense CO. That confident, wrong belief removes the perceived need to act. This is a *myth to correct*, not apathy.
5. **Psychic numbing.** Emotional response *declines* as victim counts rise; big statistics numb.

**Consequence:** facts are necessary but inert. Action = **affect × efficacy × ease**, triggered at the right moment.

## The small-numbers problem (honest reframes only)
CO deaths are objectively low (low hundreds/year); no honest source changes that, and we never inflate. Compete on a different axis:
1. **Preventability, not frequency** — nearly every death is preventable; a preventable death is a scandal at any count.
2. **The whole harm pyramid** — deaths are the tip above thousands hospitalized, tens of thousands in ERs, and many more poisoned/misdiagnosed. Counting it is honest, not inflation.
3. **The undercount** — CO is misdiagnosed as flu; the CDC notes death-investigation and ED coding vary by place. State figures as floors: "at least X, and likely more."
4. **Personal conditional risk** — not "400 ÷ 330M" but your risk *given* a fuel-burning appliance, no alarm, and winter — unknowable to you because you can't sense it.
5. **The dread asymmetry** — no warning, no second chance. A small probability with zero detectability carries outsized, legitimate weight.

## The five design mandates
1. **Affect before number (identifiable victim).** Lead with one real person; never put a stat block in the same visual unit as the victim story; scale to data on a separate beat.
2. **Threat and efficacy balanced on the same screen (EPPM).** Never show a scary fact without an easy, effective action in view. Fear without an exit backfires into denial.
3. **Make the action almost frictionless (EAST-Easy).** One primary action per page; shrink it to the smallest next step; offer implementation-intention prompts ("pick a day to test your alarm" → calendar reminder).
4. **Make acting normal (EAST-Social).** Positive, local, specific descriptive norms, peer-matched per audience. Never state a demotivating negative norm to an individual.
5. **Hit a receptive moment (EAST-Timely).** Seasonal engine (heating season, "change your clock, check your alarm," cold snaps) and life-event hooks (new lease, new home, new furnace). Make the benefit immediate and concrete ("protected tonight").

## Data-presentation rules
One number at a time; unitize ("one furnace, one family"); anchor with a concrete comparison; local beats national; story and statistics on separate planes; every figure tagged Measured/Modeled with its source; no cherry-picking or truncated axes.
