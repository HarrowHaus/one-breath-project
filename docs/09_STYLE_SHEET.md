# 09 — Editorial Style Sheet

Consistency rules for every word on the site. Voice lives in `/docs/01_VOICE_GUIDE.md`; this is the mechanics.

## Terms
- **"carbon monoxide"** spelled out on first use per page; **"CO"** acceptable after, but prefer the full term in headlines and hero copy.
- **"alarm,"** not "detector," in reader-facing copy (an alarm warns you; it's the plainer word). "Detector" is fine in technical/legal contexts.
- **"The One Breath Project"** — full name on first use; "One Breath" acceptable after.
- Say **"people who died,"** "families," "residents" — real human nouns. Avoid "fatalities," "victims" as a first choice.

## Numbers
- Every statistic shows its **source** and a **Measured/Modeled** tag. No exceptions.
- Use **"at least"** with measured counts (they're floors, not ceilings).
- Spell out small numbers in prose (one, two … nine); numerals for 10+ and all data.
- Never a truncated or misleading chart axis.

## Sourcing
- Name the source in the sentence ("the CDC," "the fire marshal's office").
- For the hero and any news-based claim, cite the outlet; confirm against the original before publishing (`{{verify:...}}`).
- Modeled figures say "estimate" nearby.

## Mechanics
- **Emergency instruction** always leads any safety-action context: "get outside and call 911."
- **Poison Control** written as `1-800-222-1222`.
- Sentence case for headings (not Title Case).
- Oxford comma. Em dashes for asides. Active voice.
- Dates: "January 2019." Money: "$20."

## Accessibility of copy
- Plain language; short sentences where they carry weight.
- Every image needs **alt text** that states what it shows and why it matters (not "image of…"). Decorative images get empty alt.
- Link text describes the destination ("Find help near me"), never "click here."

## Banned (AI tells + dark patterns in copy)
No "it's important to note," no windup conclusions, no fake urgency, no confirmshaming, no guilt CTAs. See `/docs/01_VOICE_GUIDE.md` and `/docs/02_DO_NOT_BUILD.md`.
