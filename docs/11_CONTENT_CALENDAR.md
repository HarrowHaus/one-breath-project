# 11 — Content Calendar (the timeliness engine)

Carbon monoxide risk is seasonal, so the site's prompts and campaigns are too. This is the operating schedule for the seasonal strip, the homepage emphasis, and any outreach. Timeliness is real here — never fake urgency.

## Fixed annual beats
- **Early fall (Sept–Oct):** "Before you turn on the heat" — get the furnace checked. Homepage seasonal strip → inspection.
- **Daylight saving (Nov & Mar):** "Change your clocks, change your alarm batteries." Strong homepage + share push.
- **November — CO Awareness Month:** feature the data hub and the harm pyramid; renew the pledge push.
- **Deep winter (Dec–Feb):** peak season. Emphasize alarms, sleeping-area coverage, and the "feel better when you leave the house" cue.
- **Cold snaps / winter storms / power outages (as they happen):** generator safety front and center (`/learn/generators-and-storms`). This is the highest-risk moment — surface it fast.
- **Spring daylight saving:** repeat the alarm-battery push.
- **Summer:** lower season; steady-state education, grills/boats/generators for travel and storms.

## The recency layer ("this happened again this week")
- A rotating module showing a recent, real, attributed CO incident from local news, to prove it isn't history.
- **Sourcing:** local news reporting and public incident logs. Use only attributed public facts; represent with dignity (same rules as the hero).
- **Cadence:** refresh regularly during heating season. If none is current, fall back to the anchor story rather than an old one presented as new.

## Who runs it
Content is edited through the CMS — no developer needed. Keep a simple queue of the next few beats. This calendar is the default; adjust to real events (a storm outranks the schedule).
